Registration & Disclosure

Recent registration & disclosure agency updates

EMA / CTIS Mar 2026
New CTIS Sponsor FAQ document
EMA has published a new FAQ document for CTIS sponsors, compiled from questions raised at CTIS events and via the Service Desk. It covers two areas not fully addressed in the Sponsor Handbook: IMPD-Q only applications and CSR content requirements for CTIS submission. The FAQ replaces all previous CTIS Q&A documents. For full details, see the EMA Clinical Trials Highlights Newsletter.
EMA / CTIS Mar 2026
Sponsor Handbook updated to v6.2
The CTIS Sponsor Handbook has been updated to v6.2. Key changes include references to the new Sponsor FAQ document, clarification on using the update icon rather than the add document button when drafting a modification, and specification of the mandatory fields required when requesting a trial extension of more than two years via Substantial Modification. For full details, see the EMA Clinical Trials Highlights Newsletter.
US / CT.gov 18 Mar 2026
ClinicalTrials.gov search improvements
Two new topics have been added to the Studies by Topic feature on the Search Results page. Users can now view and filter results by Sponsors/Collaborators, Conditions, or Intervention/Treatment. The Search Details feature has also been updated, allowing users to view results organised by search area and synonyms. For full details, see ClinicalTrials.gov News and Updates.

Requirements at a glance

EU / EEA — CTIS
Registration
SystemCTIS — euclinicaltrials.eu
RegulationRegulation (EU) No 536/2014 (CTR)
MandatoryMandatory for interventional clinical trials on medicinal products conducted under the EU CTR in the EU / EEA
In forceCTIS live from 31 Jan 2022; from 31 Jan 2025 all continuing or new EU / EEA IMP trials must be under the CTR / CTIS framework
Apply byCTA must be submitted and authorised before trial start
CategoriesCat. 1: Phase I / pharmaceutical development · Cat. 2: Phase I/II integrated, Phase II, Phase III · Cat. 3: Phase III–IV integrated, Phase IV, low-interventional
Results posting
Summary of resultsWithin 12 months from end of trial in all EU / EEA Member States concerned — all categories, no exemptions
Lay summary12 months from end of trial; 6 months for paediatric trials and/or trials in a PIP context
CSR3Only where the trial was intended to support an MAA in the EU / EEA. Due within 30 days after the MA is granted, the procedure is completed, or the MAA is withdrawn
Assessment timelines
ValidationUp to 10 days
Assessment Part I45 days (30 days for low-interventional trials)
Assessment Part IIPer MSC — runs in parallel with Part I
RFI response1Up to 10 days (validation); up to 12 days (assessment)
Transparency / public disclosure
Cat. 2 & 3 triggerFirst MSC decision on the CTA
Cat. 2 & 3 documentsProtocol, synopsis, patient-facing documents, SmPC (if available), ICF/PIS, recruitment arrangements
Cat. 1 adultsProtocol / synopsis / patient-facing documents publish 30 months after EU / EEA end of trial
Cat. 1 paediatric / PIPProtocol / synopsis / patient-facing documents publish with final summary results when submitted in CTIS
Published versionOnly the latest post-decision version is published; superseded RFI-response versions are not published
Key watchouts
SUSARs2Submit exclusively via EudraVigilance — fatal/life-threatening 7 days; all other 15 days. Never via CTIS.
Grandfathered trialsTrials authorised under EudraCT before the transition deadline remain on EudraCT only
PIP / Art. 46PIP trials with EU/EEA sites: register in CTIS and attach PDCO opinion. PIP/Art. 46 trials exclusively outside EEA: register in EudraCT Third Country File — not CTIS

1 CTIS does not send automatic RFI email notifications. Monitor Notices & alerts in the sponsor workspace after every submission.

2 SUSARs must never be submitted via CTIS. EudraVigilance is the only submission route.

3 The CSR deadline is tied to the MA outcome, not to the EoT notification. Trials not intended to support an MAA do not require a CSR submission in CTIS.
