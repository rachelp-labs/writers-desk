ClinicalTrials.gov Decision Tree

Determine registration and results submission obligations for US-based or FDA-regulated trials under FDAAA 801 / 42 CFR Part 11. Covers both Applicable Clinical Trials (ACTs) and NIH-funded studies.

Step 1
Is this an Applicable Clinical Trial (ACT)? ACTs under FDAAA 801 / 42 CFR §11.10: generally Phase II–IV drug/biologic/device trials with US sites or under FDA IND/IDE.
ACT
Step 2
Select the stage
ACT›Registration
Registration — ACT
⏱ Deadline
Within 21 days of first participant enrolment — not first patient screened. Registration must be submitted (not just initiated). (42 CFR §11.28)
Required Data Fields
  • Primary and secondary outcome measures (pre-specified before enrolment)
  • Eligibility criteria (inclusion/exclusion)
  • Study design, phase, and allocation
  • Sponsor and responsible party contact details
  • Recruitment status (must be kept current throughout)
  • Full protocol upload (required for NIH-funded trials)
IND / FDA Note
IND application to FDA (21 CFR Part 312) is a separate, parallel obligation for unapproved drugs/biologics. CT.gov registration and IND are independent — both may be required.
Penalty
Non-compliance: up to $12,897/day civil penalty. FDA and NIH have increased enforcement since 2022.
FDAAA §80142 CFR §11.28FDA Guidance Jan 2023
ACT›Results Submission
Results — ACT
⏱ Results Deadline
Within 12 months of the Primary Completion Date (PCD).
PCD = the date the LAST participant is measured for the primary outcome. This is NOT the same as last patient visit or database lock. (42 CFR §11.44)
What to Submit
Structured results data entered directly into CT.gov results module. Adverse event table mandatory — must include all serious and other AEs meeting reporting thresholds.
Certificate of Delay
Available in limited circumstances (e.g. pending marketing application). Requires certification to FDA/NIH. Does not exempt — delays only.
Common PCD Mistake
Sponsors frequently miscalculate PCD. PCD is the date the last participant is measured for the PRIMARY outcome — not last visit, not database lock.
42 CFR §11.4442 U.S.C. §282(j)(5)(A)FDA Guidance Jan 2023
Phase I
Phase I — Generally Exempt
ℹ Phase I Drug Trials
Phase I drug trials are generally exempt from mandatory ACT registration under FDAAA 801. Voluntary registration is strongly recommended for ICMJE journal publication compliance — register before first enrolment in any WHO ICTRP primary registry.

No mandatory results submission for non-ACT Phase I trials. Voluntary posting recommended.
Exceptions
NIH-funded Phase I trials: mandatory regardless of ACT status (NIH Policy 2017).
Device trials: Phase I device trials under IDE may still qualify as ACTs — confirm with regulatory team.
FDAAA §80142 CFR Part 11ICMJE Recommendations
NIH-funded
NIH Policy 2017
NIH Policy Requirements
NIH-funded clinical trials must register and post results in CT.gov regardless of ACT status under the NIH Policy on Dissemination of NIH-Funded Clinical Trial Information (2017).

Registration: before first participant enrolment.
Results: within 12 months of PCD (same as ACT).
Protocol upload: required for NIH-funded trials.
ℹ Interaction with FDAAA
NIH Policy applies independently of FDAAA 801. A NIH-funded Phase I trial that is not an ACT still must register and post results under NIH Policy.
NIH Policy 201742 CFR §11.44
ACT Determination
ACT Determination
ACT Criteria (all must apply)
Under FDAAA 801 / 42 CFR §11.10, a study is an ACT if it meets all of the following:

  • It is a clinical investigation of a drug, biologic, or device subject to FDA regulation
  • It has one or more US sites or is conducted under FDA IND/IDE
  • It is a controlled trial with one or more arms (or a single-arm device trial)
  • Phase II, III, or IV (drug/biologic) OR any phase for devices under IDE

If all apply → register as ACT. If not → voluntary registration, unless NIH-funded.
Grey Areas
Observational studies are generally NOT ACTs. Expanded access protocols are NOT ACTs. When uncertain, voluntary registration is always safe and ICMJE-compliant.
