Clinical Trial Protocol For publication Cat. 2 & 3: at first MSC decision · Cat. 1 paediatric/PIP: with final results · Cat. 1 adult-only: 30 months after end of trial
▼
Upload note
TWO versions required. Upload the "for publication" version first, with personal data and CCI removed, then upload the linked "not for publication" full version. Remove file metadata (file properties / author fields) from both versions before upload.

Publication timing by category: Cat. 2 & 3 — at first MSC decision. Cat. 1 paediatric/PIP — published together with the final summary of results, as soon as results are submitted in CTIS. Cat. 1 adult-only — 30 months after end of trial in the EU/EEA.
Redaction guide
Redact from published version
  • Subject/patient identifiers and personal data
  • Private contact details of investigators (functional/professional details should be retained)
  • Signatures (must always be removed from published version)
  • Commercially confidential manufacturing details
  • Unpublished proprietary assay or biomarker methods
  • IP-sensitive compound or formulation information (pre-authorisation)
Retain in published version
  • Protocol title, version date, and CTIS/EudraCT number
  • Primary and secondary objectives
  • Inclusion and exclusion criteria
  • Study design, endpoints, and statistical methods
  • Investigational product name (INN preferred)
  • Names of principal investigators and head of clinic/institution
  • Professional/functional contact details of principal investigators
  • Sponsor name and functional contact details

ClinicalTrials.gov Decision Tree

Determine registration and results submission obligations for US-based or FDA-regulated trials under FDAAA 801 / 42 CFR Part 11.

Step 1
Is this an Applicable Clinical Trial (ACT)?ACT criteria (all must apply)
Under FDAAA 801 / 42 CFR Part 11, a study is generally an ACT if:
· It is a clinical investigation of a drug, biologic, or device subject to FDA regulation
· It has the required US/FDA nexus (for example, one or more US sites or study under FDA IND/IDE)
· For drugs/biologics: it is an interventional study, phase other than Phase I
· For devices: it is a prospective controlled study of health outcomes, excluding small feasibility studies
ACT
Step 2
Select the stage
ACT›Registration
Registration — ACT
Registration Deadline
Within 21 days after first participant enrolment — not first patient screened.
Required Data Fields
  • Descriptive: brief title, brief summary, study design, study phase, primary disease/condition, intervention name(s) and type, primary and secondary outcome measure information, study start date, primary completion date
  • Recruitment: eligibility criteria, sex/gender, age limits, accepts healthy volunteers, overall recruitment status
  • Location and contact: name of sponsor, responsible party by official title, facility information
  • Administrative: unique protocol identification number, IND or IDE number, IRB status, responsible party contact information
42 CFR §11.2442 CFR §11.2842 CFR §11.22(b) ACT Checklist
ACT›Results Submission
Results — ACT
Results Deadline
Within 12 months of the PCD, unless a permitted delay or certification applies.
PCD = the date the final participant was examined or received an intervention for final collection of data for the primary outcome.
Required Results Information
All submitted via the CT.gov results module:
  • Participant flow: progress of participants through the trial by arm, including numbers who started and completed
  • Demographics and baseline characteristics: by arm or comparison group and overall
  • Outcomes and statistical analyses: primary and secondary outcome measure data by arm, including results of statistical analyses
  • Adverse events: required AE table
  • Protocol: full protocol in a common electronic format
  • Statistical analysis plan: if separate from the protocol
  • Administrative: results point of contact, certain agreements between principal investigator and sponsor
Common PCD Mistake
PCD is the date the final participant is examined or receives an intervention for the PRIMARY outcome — not last visit, not database lock.
42 CFR §11.4442 CFR §11.48
Not ACT
Not ACT — Generally Excluded
What This Means
Mandatory registration and results reporting under 42 CFR Part 11 do not apply. Expanded access use is expressly excluded from the definition of an applicable clinical trial under 42 CFR §11.10.
Studies Generally Excluded
  • Phase I drug trials
  • Observational studies
  • Expanded access protocols
Additional Considerations
  • Voluntary registration: permitted under 42 CFR §11.60 — may be appropriate for transparency purposes
  • ICMJE: if journal publication is planned, confirm whether prospective registration before first enrolment was required
  • Device edge cases: confirm whether the study meets the device feasibility exclusion under 42 CFR §11.22(b)
NIH-funded? Different rules apply
NIH-funded clinical trials must register and post results in CT.gov regardless of ACT status.

Registration: no later than 21 days after first participant enrolment.
Results: within 12 months of PCD.
Protocol upload: required.

For ACTs that are also NIH-funded, 42 CFR Part 11 protocol and SAP submission requirements also apply.
