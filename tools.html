Redaction guide
Redact from published version
  • Subject/patient identifiers and personal data

EudraCT / Paediatric Check
Four scenarios still require EudraCT or PDCO involvement:
  • PIP trial with EU/EEA sites: Register in CTIS. Attach PDCO opinion + PIP Compliance Statement.
  • PIP/Art.46 exclusively outside EEA: Register in EudraCT Third Country File — NOT CTIS.
  • Art.46 study (MAH-sponsored): Submit to competent authority within 6 months of completion.
  • Grandfathered trial: Remains on EudraCT only.

ClinicalTrials.gov Decision Tree

Determine registration and results submission obligations for US-based or FDA-regulated trials under FDAAA 801 / 42 CFR Part 11.

Step 1
Is this an Applicable Clinical Trial (ACT)?ACTs under FDAAA 801 / 42 CFR §11.10: generally Phase II–IV drug/biologic/device trials with US sites or under FDA IND/IDE.
ACT
Step 2
Select the stage
ACT›Registration
Registration — ACT
Deadline
Within 21 days of first participant enrolment — not first patient screened.
Required Data Fields
  • Primary and secondary outcome measures (pre-specified before enrolment)
  • Eligibility criteria
  • Study design, phase, and allocation
  • Sponsor and responsible party contact details
  • Recruitment status (must be kept current)
  • Full protocol upload (required for NIH-funded trials)
Penalty
Non-compliance: up to $12,897/day civil penalty.
FDAAA §80142 CFR §11.28FDA Guidance Jan 2023
ACT›Results Submission
Results — ACT
Results Deadline
Within 12 months of the PCD.
PCD = the date the LAST participant is measured for the primary outcome. This is NOT the same as last patient visit or database lock.
What to Submit
Structured results data entered directly into CT.gov results module. AE table mandatory.
Common PCD Mistake
PCD is the date the last participant is measured for the PRIMARY outcome — not last visit, not database lock.

Phase I Drug Trials
Generally exempt from mandatory ACT registration under FDAAA 801. Voluntary registration strongly recommended for ICMJE journal publication compliance.

ACT Determination
ACT Determination
ACT Criteria (all must apply)
Under FDAAA 801 / 42 CFR §11.10, a study is an ACT if:
  • Clinical investigation of a drug, biologic, or device subject to FDA regulation
  • One or more US sites or under FDA IND/IDE
  • Controlled trial with one or more arms
  • Phase II, III, or IV (drug/biologic) or any phase for devices under IDE

If all apply → register as ACT.
Grey Areas
Observational studies are generally NOT ACTs. Expanded access protocols are NOT ACTs. When uncertain, voluntary registration is always safe and ICMJE-compliant.
